Remind them to submit to the rulers and authorities, to obey, to be ready for every good work,
that they do not speak ill of anybody, that they are not contentious but kind, showing all consideration for all men.
Because once we were also foolish, disobedient, lost. We were enslaved by various passions and pleasures, living in malice and envy. We were abhorrent, hating each other.
But when the goodness of God our Savior and his love for men was manifested,
He saved us, not by the works of justice that we had done, but according to his mercy; through the washing of regeneration and the renewal of the Holy Spirit
that he poured out on us abundantly through Jesus Christ our Savior.
And this so that, justified by his grace, we become heirs according to the hope of eternal life.
Faithful is this word. About these things, I want you to speak out firmly so that those who have believed in God may try to do good works. These things are good and useful to men.
But avoid foolish strife, genealogy, controversy, and debate about the law; because they take advantage of nothing and are futile.
After one warning and another, he rejects the man who causes divisions,
knowing that such a man has perverted and sinned, having condemned himself.
When I send Artemas or Tychicus to you, try to come to Nicopolis for I have decided to spend the winter there.
Send Zenas, teacher of the law, and Apollos so that they do not lack anything.
And let our people learn to dedicate themselves to good works in cases of need, so that they are not without fruit.
All who are with me greet you. Said hello to the people who love you in the faith. Grace be with you all [a].
