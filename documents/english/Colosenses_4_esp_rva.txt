Masters, do what is just and equitable with your servants, knowing that you also have a master in heaven.
Always persevere in prayer, watching over it with thanksgiving.
At the same time, pray also for us so that the Lord opens a door for us to speak to communicate the mystery of Christ, for which I am still in prison.
Pray that I will present it clearly, as I need to speak.
Walk wisely with those outside, redeeming time.
May your word be always pleasant, seasoned with salt, so that you know how it is convenient for you to respond to each one.
All my affairs will be made known to you by Tychicus, beloved brother, faithful minister, and fellow servant in the Lord.
I am sending him to you for this purpose: so that you may know our affairs [b] and that he may encourage your hearts.
I send him with Onesimus, the faithful and beloved brother, who is one of you. They will inform you of everything that happens here.
Aristarco, prisoner with me, greet you; Mark, Barnabas's cousin (they have already received instructions about him; if he comes to you, receive him)
and Jesus, called Justus. They are the only ones in circumcision who are co-workers with me in the kingdom of God and who have comforted me.
Epaphras, who is one of you, servant of Christ, greets you always in prayer, greets you so that you may stand firm as mature men and completely surrendered to all the will of God.
Because I bear witness to him, that he is very zealous for you, for those of Laodicea and for those of Hierapolis.
Lucas, the beloved physician, and Demas greet you.
Greet the brothers who are in Laodicea: Nymph and the church that is in her house.
When this letter has been read among you, have it read also in the church of the Laodiceans; and that of Laodicea, read it also.
Say to Archippus: "Take care of the ministry you have received from the Lord, so that you may fulfill it."
Now, the greeting of my own hand, from Pablo. Remember my prisons. Grace be with you [c].
