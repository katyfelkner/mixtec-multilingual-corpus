Remind them to submit to the rulers and authorities, to obey, to be ready for every good work.
Do not defame anyone, that they are not quarrelsome, but kind, showing all meekness towards all men.
For we, too, were once fools, rebellious, astray, slaves of lusts and various delights, living in malice and envy, abhorrent and abhorring one another.
But when the goodness of God our Savior was manifested, and his love for men,
He saved us, not by works of righteousness that we had done, but by his mercy, by the washing of regeneration and renewal by the Holy Spirit,
He who poured out on us abundantly through Jesus Christ our Savior,
so that, justified by his grace, we might become heirs according to the hope of eternal life.
This is a faithful word, and I want you to insist firmly on these things, so that those who believe in God may try to do good works. These things are good and useful to men.
But avoid foolish questions, and genealogies, and contentions and debates about the law, because they are useless and futile.
To the man who causes divisions, after one warning and the other, reject him,
knowing that such has gone astray, and sins, being condemned by his own judgment.
When I send you to Artemas, or Tychicus, try to come to me at Nicopolis, because there I have determined to spend the winter.
Zenas, doctor of the law, and Apollos, route them diligently, trying to ensure that nothing is lacking.
And also learn ours to engage in good works for cases of need, so that they are not without fruit.
All who are with me greet you. Said hello to the people who love you in the faith. Grace be with you all. Amen.
