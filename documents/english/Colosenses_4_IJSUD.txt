Masters, do what is just and equitable with your servants, knowing that you also have a Master in heaven.
Persevere in prayer, watching in it with thanksgiving,
also praying together for us, so that the Lord opens the door for us to speak, in order to proclaim the mystery of Christ, for which I am also a prisoner,
so that it manifests as I must speak.
Walk in wisdom towards strangers, taking advantage of time.
Let your word always be with grace, seasoned with salt, so that you know how you should respond to each one.
Tychicus, beloved brother and faithful minister and fellow servant in the Lord, will make all my affairs known to you.
to whom I have sent you for this very purpose, to know how you are, and to comfort your hearts,
with Onesimus, beloved and faithful brother, who is one of you. Everything that happens here, they will let you know.
Aristarchus, my fellow prisoner, greets you, and Mark, the nephew of Barnabas (about whom you have received instructions; if he came to you, receive him),
and Jesus, the one called Righteous; These are the only ones in circumcision who help me in the kingdom of God, and who have been a comfort to me.
Epaphras, who is one of you, servant of Christ, greets you, always praying earnestly for you in his prayers, so that you remain firm, perfect and complete in all that God wants.
For I bear witness of him, that he is very zealous for you, and for those in Laodicea and those in Hierapolis.
Lucas, the beloved physician, and Demas greet you.
Greet the brothers who are in Laodicea, and Nymphs and the church that is in their house.
And when this letter has been read among you, make it also read in the church of the Laodiceans, and that of Laodicea you also read.
And say to Archippus: See that you fulfill the ministry that you have received from the Lord.
This greeting is from my own hand, from Pablo. Remember my prisons. May the grace be with you. Amen.
